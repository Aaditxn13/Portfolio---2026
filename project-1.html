OVERVIEW

We were given a rare brief — take a product that millions of people use, throw out everything, and start over. New name, new code, new design. No legacy constraints.

PayZapp was HDFC Bank's digital wallet — one of the earliest in India. But years of incremental additions had left it feeling patchy: inconsistent UI, friction-heavy flows, and an onboarding that dropped users before they ever made a transaction. The decision was made to not patch it, but to rebuild it entirely — new brand identity, a ground-up design system, and a full rewrite in Flutter. This is Zapp Account.

What we inherited.

"No visual hierarchy. Actions buried. Every screen felt like it was designed in isolation — because it was, over years of feature additions with no system underneath."

The core problems weren't just visual. The wallet lacked a clear information hierarchy — users couldn't tell at a glance what their balance was, what they could do, or how to get started. Onboarding was long and confusing, leading to high drop-off before first transaction. And the product had no consistent interaction language — every feature felt bolted on.

The answer wasn't a redesign. It was a rebuild. A new brand — Zapp Account — built ground up with a proper component system, a new visual language, and a codebase written entirely in Flutter. For the first time, design and engineering could move together.

[ Zapp Account Hero Spread — Home · Payments · Profile ]

The design direction was built around one principle: clarity at every step. The home screen leads with balance — the one number users care about most. Actions are surfaced immediately. The visual language is clean but warm, avoiding the cold utility feel of most fintech apps. Typography does heavy lifting; colour is used deliberately, not decoratively.

VIDEO KYC

Alongside the product rebuild, we introduced Video KYC into the onboarding — a first for the wallet. In India, KYC is a regulatory requirement to unlock full wallet functionality, but it's historically been one of the biggest friction points in fintech onboarding. The challenge was designing a flow that felt calm and guided, despite the technical and compliance constraints it had to work within.

Reflections.

Rebuilding ground up is rare — and it's a test as much as an opportunity. Without legacy constraints, every decision is yours to justify, which means you have to be sharper about the why behind everything. The biggest lesson from this project was that a clean slate doesn't automatically produce a better product. The research, the hard conversations about what the product actually needed to do, and the willingness to say no to features that didn't belong — that's what made the difference. The Flutter rewrite also changed how I think about the design-engineering relationship. When the tools match the ambition, you stop negotiating your design away.
